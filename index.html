Iconsets: Nature

As part of a university project, I was tasked with designing two distinct icon sets in different styles. The challenge was to translate given terms related to "nature" into visually coherent and expressive icons. This project required a deep understanding of stylistic differentiation, consistency in design, and effective visual communication. Through this process, I explored various artistic approaches, experimented with forms and line work, and refined my ability to create icons that are both aesthetically pleasing and functional.

For the first set, I chose Pixel Art, a digital art style characterized by its blocky, low-resolution aesthetic reminiscent of early video games and computer graphics. Working within the constraints of a limited pixel grid forced me to focus on clarity and simplicity while maintaining recognizability. Each icon was meticulously crafted, with careful attention to color palettes, dithering techniques, and minimalistic shapes to convey the essence of natural elements. This style demanded a balance between readability and artistic expression, as even a single misplaced pixel could disrupt the overall harmony. By embracing the nostalgic charm of Pixel Art, I also learned how to evoke emotion and context through deliberate stylization, reinforcing how deliberate limitations can enhance creativity.

For the second set, I shifted to a loose, scribbly drawing style, inspired by the spontaneous energy of hand-drawn sketches and children‚Äôs book illustrations. Unlike the rigid precision of Pixel Art, this approach celebrated imperfection, distributing color beyond the outlines whilst creating a more skeuomorphistic look in general. This stile challenged me to create icons that would, in theory, be understood by children, facing me with the need of simplicity in terms of used shapes and objects. It was an interesting new approach that helped me gain a deeper understanding of how important it is to reflect upon certain factors when creating designs that target specific audiences.

Screendesign: taddle

For this personal project, I designed a complete screen interface for "taddle", a chat app focused on delivering both functionality and flair. The project involved creating a unique brand identity, including logo design, as well as crafting user-friendly and visually engaging screens. I aimed to combine seamless navigation with a modern, approachable aesthetic, ensuring the app‚Äôs design reflected its personality while maintaining an intuitive user experience. This project allowed me to merge creative design with practical solutions, resulting in an app interface that is both stylish and easy to use.

Further into detail, my focus was to ensure consitency among the UI elements. This meant that I had to be mindful about aspects like color, typography and iconography. In consequence, I created a styleguide that summed up all the important aspects of the direction I wanted to go for, reaching from the palette to the radii applied on the icons I created for the app. This ensured that the app's appearance is taylored to its target audience and their preferences.

Animation: Loading

During my second semester, I worked on creating a loading animation in After Effects, where I learned the significance of easing in animation to create smooth and natural motion. For this project, I chose the university's logo as my central element and aimed to transform it into a loading animation that would seamlessly loop. The challenge was to ensure the animation was both visually engaging and easy to understand, while maintaining a fluid rhythm. This project deepened my appreciation for animation principles and the subtlety of timing in creating intuitive user experiences.

Redesign: Printer UI

As part of a practical design initiative, I undertook the challenge of redesigning my university‚Äôs printer interface, which suffered from a cluttered layout, unintuitive navigation, and an outdated visual style. The existing UI overwhelmed users with dense buttons, unclear hierarchies, and inefficient workflows, making even basic tasks like double-sided printing or selecting paper trays needlessly frustrating.

In my redesign, I focused on creating a clean, modern interface that prioritized user experience and accessibility. I simplified the layout by grouping related functions, using clear icons and labels, and implementing a more intuitive navigation flow. The new design also incorporated a visually appealing yet comforting color scheme and typography, enhancing the overall aesthetic while ensuring clarity and usability. By addressing the pain points of the original interface, I aimed to create a more efficient and enjoyable printing experience for users.

App Concept: CareScope

As part of a university project, we were tasked with developing a digital solution to support people living with dementia. The process began with in-depth research into the illness, its symptoms, progression, and the everyday challenges it poses for both patients and caregivers. We explored medical studies, caregiver testimonials, and existing assistive technologies to build a solid foundation. From there, we moved into concept development, ideating potential features that could meaningfully support independence and remote caregiving. The design phase followed, where we focused on creating a calm, non-overstimulating interface that would feel approachable and comforting for users with cognitive impairments.

The final concept was a companion app connected to a network of smart devices installed in the patient's home. These devices monitored daily routines, ensured safety, and allowed caregivers to stay informed and provide assistance remotely. Our goal was to strike a balance between autonomy and oversight, enabling dementia patients to maintain a sense of normalcy while reducing the constant physical and emotional demands on caregivers. We conducted user testing to refine the interface, paying close attention to clarity, simplicity, and emotional tone. The experience taught us how important it is to design not only for usability, but for dignity and emotional well-being, especially in sensitive healthcare contexts.
